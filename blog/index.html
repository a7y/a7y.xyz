You should adhere to ISO 8601

If you aren’t already sold on this date format, let me elaborate. The International Organization for Standardization, or ISO, is a committee that sets forth standards intended for 163 countries. You undoubtedly use something everyday that adheres to some specification provided by the organization. For example: film speed is defined by ISO 6, 2240, and 5800. And the file extension .ISO (used for disk images) pays homage to ISO 9660. Country (“us”, “uk”) and language codes (“en”, “es”) stem from ISO 3166 and 639 respectively.
